Asteroid Hunter

Automatically finds moving asteroids in telescope images. Upload a set of frames of the same patch of sky and let the pipeline track what moves.

Import your images → Hunt by ear 🔊 →

Free · no sign-up · nothing to install

1

Upload your frames

Add 3–10 telescope images (.fits, or a .zip of them) of the same patch of sky. Four or more works best.

2

The pipeline scans

It lines up the frames and looks for the one thing that shifts position while the stars stay put.

3

See what moves

Blink and zoom through the frames, and any known asteroids get named with their orbits.

  • ✔ Works with .fits or .zip
  • ✔ Identifies known asteroids
  • ✔ Blink & zoom viewer
  • ✔ Results in ~1–2 min

Built to be explored by everyone

🔊

Detect by ear — sonification. An asteroid's motion is turned into sound: the closer your cursor is to the moving object, the louder it gets. With full keyboard control and spoken guidance, blind and low-vision users can hunt asteroids by hearing.

🪐

Orbital viewer. See a named asteroid's real path around the Sun, drawn from its known orbital data.
